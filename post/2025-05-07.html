AI Buddies vs. Human Friends – Future of Companionship

Topic Analysis

This JAM topic explores the evolving relationship between artificial intelligence and human social interaction, questioning whether AI companions can replace or complement human friendships in the future.

Potential Arguments:

  • For AI Companions: Always available, personalized interactions, no judgment, 24/7 support
  • For Human Friends: Emotional depth, shared experiences, physical presence, genuine empathy
  • Hybrid Approach: AI could supplement human relationships but not replace them entirely

This impromptu speech demonstrates how to structure thoughts quickly on a complex topic using the public speaking framework below.

Public Speaking Made Simple

A step-by-step guide to effective impromptu speaking, adapted from the student's guide to public speaking.

1. Set Your Compass

  • Goal: Decide exactly why you're speaking
  • Write one sentence that starts with "After my talk, my classmates will..." (e.g., "...understand why human connection remains essential in the AI age")
  • Circle the single most important verb ("know," "believe," "try") – that becomes your purpose

2. Know Your Crew

  • Audience: Friends, not judges
  • List 3 things your audience already understands about the topic (e.g., "AI is advancing rapidly," "Loneliness is increasing," "People use social media heavily")
  • List 3 blanks they probably have (e.g., "How AI companions actually work," "Psychological effects of AI friendships," "Long-term social implications")
  • Aim to fill just those blanks - nothing extra

3. Pick One Big Idea

  • Focus: One core takeaway sticks better than ten facts
  • Sum up your idea in 10 words or fewer (e.g., "AI complements but can't replace human emotional bonds")
  • Test it on a friend; if they get it, move on

4. Build a Simple Skeleton (O-B-C)

Part What to do Tip Time (for 5-min talk)
Opening Hook + reason to listen Question, statistic, or mini-story 45 sec
Body 2-3 supporting points Use PEEL – Point, Explain, Example, Link 3 min
Close Signal the end + call to action "Remember..." or "So next time..." 45 sec

PEEL Method Explained

Point: State your main idea clearly

Explain: Provide reasoning or evidence

Example: Give a concrete illustration

Link: Connect back to your main argument

5. Add a Personal Touch

  • Insert one story or analogy from campus life (e.g., "This reminds me of when our group project almost failed because we only communicated through chatbots")
  • Keep it under 60 seconds; stories make data memorable

6. Practice the 3-Pass Method

  • Pass 1 – Quiet Read: Read notes silently to check flow
  • Pass 2 – Mirror Talk: Say it aloud while watching posture
  • Pass 3 – Record & Review: Use phone video; look for pace (120–150 wpm) and filler words ("um")
  • Do all three passes; total time ≈ 15 minutes

7. Calm the Nerves (30-Second Routine)

  • Breathe: Deep inhale through nose, slow exhale through mouth
  • Power pose: Feet shoulder-width, shoulders back
  • Positive cue: Whisper your goal sentence
  • Walk up: Make eye contact with one friendly face first

8. Deliver With the 3 Vs

  • Voice: Loud enough for back row, vary tone
  • Verbal: Simple words; avoid jargon unless explained
  • Visual: Hands visible, purposeful gestures, natural smile

9. Handle Sudden Blanks (JAM-style)

  • Pause, breathe, restate last sentence to buy 3-4 seconds
  • If stuck, ask the audience a yes/no question – regains flow

10. Collect Feedback & Iterate

  • Ask two friends: "One thing clear? One thing to improve?"
  • Note it in a "Speaker Log" (Google Keep/Notion)
  • Apply one fix next time—constant 1% upgrades
